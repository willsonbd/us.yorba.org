Dan Campbell believes Jared Goff is a better quarterback for him than he was playing for Sean McVay\u2019s Los Angeles Rams.Look at Dan Campbell driving the Jared Goff hype train, man!Together, the Detroit Lions<\/a> are well on their way towards building something special. After narrowly missing out on the NFC playoffs a year ago, one could argue that Detroit is the favorite to come out of the NFC North this season. A big reason for that has been the play of Goff. Campbell went as far to say Goff is a better quarterback now than with the Sean McVay Los Angeles Rams<\/a>.While I\u2019m sure a lot of it has to do working with offensive coordinator Ben Johnson and quarterbacks coach Mark Brunell, it\u2019s not like Goff was a scrub coming out of Cal. He played for Sonny Dykes and was the No. 1 overall pick by the Rams in the 2016 NFL Draft<\/a> for a reason!Here is what Campbell had to say at the podium on Tuesday when speaking with the media.\u201cHis confidence has really grown \u2026 he\u2019s a better quarterback than he was [in L.A.], in my opinion\u2026 we\u2019ve come lightyears ahead of where he was two years ago when we started teaching him protections.\u201dThis is the entire clip of Campbell speaking with the Lions media about Goff and other big topics.The best part is the Lions are finally pulling in the same direction. It had been a while, to be honest.Dan Campbell feels Jared Goff is a better quarterback now with the Detroit LionsLook. I love everything about this comment from Campbell. It is fantastic. Goff always had talent. I remember watching him sling the pill around for Cal when the Golden Bears weren\u2019t the least bit good and I thought to myself, \u201cOh, there is something here for sure!\u201d Unfortunately, he played for the worst offenses of my lifetime on the 2016 Rams for Jeff Fisher. That would\u2019ve ruined anyone.After not knowing where the sun went, Goff emerged as a Pro Bowl-caliber passer for the Rams for the next four years. Sadly, it wasn\u2019t good enough for McVay, so he convinced owner Stan Kroenke to trade for fellow Georgia boy Matthew Stafford. Together, McVay and Stafford won a Super Bowl<\/a>. While the Lions stunk two years ago, they have the better ceiling \u2026 because of Goff.Yes, Detroit may have drafted Hendon Hooker out of Tennessee, but I am nowhere near as high on him as I was Goff coming out of Cal. He may be the future of Lions football, or he may just be a backup. Regardless, look for Goff to continue to grow in Johnson\u2019s offense for as long as he remains in Detroit, as well as build on his rapport with Johnson\u2019s potential successor in Brunell.See, Goff throws a beautiful spiral, but does need a savvy offensive mind to set him up for success to make plays down the field. In time, Goff will grow and mature into his late 20s, now that the cerebral part of his game has caught up to his natural gifts as a passer. Either way, I don\u2019t think we\u2019ve seen the best football out of Goff yet.
